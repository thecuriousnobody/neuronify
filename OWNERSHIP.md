# How Neuronify is built & owned

Neuronify is built in public, in Peoria — on purpose. A civic nervous system
should be transparent about how it thinks. So here's the honest breakdown, so
anyone can use, learn from, and contribute to it with a clear conscience.

## What's open (this repository)

The code in this repo is released under the **MIT License** (see `LICENSE`).
Use it, fork it, learn from it, build on it, run your own version — commercial
or not. We'd genuinely love that.

## What's reserved (not part of this open repo)

A few things are **not** covered by the open-source license and remain with the
project's maintainers — today, Rajeev Kumar and a small team forming in Peoria,
Illinois:

- The **"Neuronify" name and brand**.
- The **hosted service at neuronify.ai** and its operation.
- Any **resident submissions, city data, and session data** gathered through the
  hosted service. Submissions are anonymous and treated as community signal —
  not a product to be resold.
- The **connector / "synapse" network layer** — the curated graph of people,
  knowledge, and relationships — and its curation.
- **Commercial deployments and partnerships** with cities.

In short: the **engine is open**; the **operation, the brand, the data, and the
network** are ours to steward.

## Contributing

By contributing to this repository, you agree your contributions are provided
under the same MIT License (inbound = outbound). Build in the open, be kind, and
remember the whole point:

**Contribution is membership.** A city that thinks of itself as one organism —
every resident a neuron, every connection a synapse. Welcome aboard.
